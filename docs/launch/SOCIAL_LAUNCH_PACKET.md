### 5 Authority-Building Posts

**Post 1 — Statute Deep Dive**

> FCRA §611 gives consumers the right to dispute inaccurate information directly with credit bureaus. Bureaus have 30 days to investigate (45 if you provide additional information).
>
> If they can't verify the item, they must remove it.
>
> Most people don't know this exists. That's a structural information gap.
>
> We're building software to close that gap. 605b.ai

**Post 2 — Self-Service Model**

> Traditional credit dispute services act as an intermediary — sending letters, managing communication, charging monthly.
>
> We built 605b.ai as self-service software instead. You generate the letters. You review them. You send them.
>
> Same underlying process. Full transparency. One-time payment.

**Post 3 — FTC/CFPB Context**

> The FTC and CFPB have increased enforcement around consumer credit services that make misleading outcome promises.
>
> 605b.ai makes no outcome promises. We provide software tools and educational guidance. You are the operator.
>
> Transparency is a feature, not a liability.

**Post 4 — Technical Architecture**

> When a user uploads a credit report to 605b.ai, the PDF is processed for analysis and not retained as a document. Only the analysis results the user chooses to save are stored to their account.
>
> No credit report PDFs stored as retrievable files. No data sold. No data shared with third-party marketing.
>
> Privacy-first architecture isn't optional when handling sensitive financial data.

**Post 5 — Identity Theft Statistics**

> FTC received 1.4 million identity theft reports in 2023. The median resolution time exceeds 6 months.
>
> FCRA §605B gives victims the right to block fraudulent information from their credit reports. Exercising that right requires specific documentation: affidavits, police reports, FTC reports, and structured correspondence.
>
> 605b.ai organizes that entire documentation workflow.

### 5 Educational Tweets

1. `Under FCRA §611, credit bureaus have 30 days to investigate a dispute after receiving your letter. If they can't verify the information, they must remove or modify it. This isn't a hack. It's federal law.`

2. `You can get your credit reports from all three bureaus for free at AnnualCreditReport.com. You're entitled to one free report per bureau per year, plus additional free reports if you've been denied credit.`

3. `Dispute letters should cite specific statutes, reference specific accounts, and include relevant documentation. Generic "please investigate" letters get generic responses.`

4. `If a bureau verifies a disputed item, you have the right under FCRA §611(a)(7) to request their method of verification. This forces them to disclose how they confirmed the information.`

5. `FCRA §623 allows you to dispute directly with the furnisher (creditor or collector), not just the bureau. Different statute, different obligations, different documentation requirements.`

### 5 Identity Theft Awareness Tweets

1. `If someone opened accounts in your name, FCRA §605B gives you the right to block that information from your credit report. This requires an FTC Identity Theft Report — not just a dispute letter.`

2. `Identity theft victims should file reports in this order: (1) FTC Identity Theft Report at IdentityTheft.gov, (2) Local police report, (3) Fraud alerts with all three bureaus, (4) Individual account disputes.`

3. `A fraud alert is not the same as a credit freeze. Fraud alerts require creditors to verify your identity before opening new accounts. Credit freezes prevent access entirely. Both are free. Use both.`

4. `Identity theft documentation takes time. Average resolution exceeds 6 months. Having a structured workflow — affidavits, police reports, bureau correspondence, deadline tracking — is critical.`

5. `Under FCRA §605B, bureaus must block fraudulent information within 4 business days of receiving your blocking request with supporting documentation. The clock starts when they receive your complete submission.`
